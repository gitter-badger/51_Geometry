By the Apollonius theorem, in any triangle, the \PMlinkescapetext{lengths} $m_a$, $m_b$, $m_c$ of the \PMlinkname{medians}{Median} of opposing the the sides $a$, $b$, $c$, respectively,
are
$$m_a = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{2b^2+2c^2-a^2},$$
$$m_b = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{2c^2+2a^2-b^2},$$
$$m_c = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{2a^2+2b^2-c^2}.$$
